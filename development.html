A Timeline of Internet and Technology Development

1950’s

Sputnik

The Soviet Union created the first artificial satellite in 1957. They called it Sputnik and launched it into space on October 4th 1957. The satellite was set to orbit the earth and did so continuously until January 1958 when Sputnik burned and fell back down to earth. Lasting within 3 months in space orbiting earth every 96 minutes. This set off a Space Race between the Russians and Americans.

Bell Labs

In 1958 the US Military had the company Bell Labs research a development in a modem that could read digital signal and convert it into an analogue for broadcasting over telephone lines. Bell Labs created the first released modem naming it Bell 101 Modem in 1958. It was used by the North American Aerospace Defence command or for short NORAD in their SAGE computer systems. The signals that the modem picked up from radar sites produced an image of the airspace over a wide area. This was in response of a possible air attack on America.

ARPA

Due to Sputnik happening the US Government established a Research in Technology and Military Applications facility. This was in 1958 just a year after Sputnik. The purpose of this establishment was to study and produce Hardware, Software, Algorithms, Data, Computer and Network Design, Information Processing and potentially AI applications. This is how Time-Sharing computers were formed. Time-Sharing allowed the use of computers in the same building to interact through the CPU (central processing unit) on digital computers. Time-Sharing included multiprocessing, multiprogramming, data exchange and parallel operations which increased the speed of computer systems. At the time the research of the facility was classified only to the Department of Defence Directive known as DOD.

1960’s

Packet Switching

Time-Sharing evolved into Packet Switching which meant that data was now formed into packets. This was to speed up the transfer of files over the network. When the data packets are sent separately over the network and attach again once they reach their destination. The key for this was known as Store and Forward. Multiple source destinations allowed the packets to go to more than one destination with each destination receiving the full packet of the file. With multiple users sending packets over the same network, the source would sometimes get an overload of packets causing a stop at which point the user sending the file had the option of another source to use with less packets travelling on and would still reach its destination.

ASCII

The 1960’s saw the development of the American Standard Code for Information Interchange or for short ASCII. ASCII is an encoding formula to represent text and control characters in digital format. It originated from Telegraph and Morse coding into a 7bit binary code. It allows communications and data exchange across computer systems. It includes letters, numbers and punctuation. Control characters allowed commands like start/end text, backspace, shift in/out and other controls. ASCII had over 128 characters as commands for symbols. The system differentiates the decimal and hexadecimal numbers in order to produce the code being entered and knowing its appointed character.

WAN

The first Wide Area Network created was SAGE.

ARPANet

Lawrence Roberts worked for ARPA and in1969 he created the first packet-switching which connected the first two computers. The network expanded in1973 and had spread to 30 institutions, universities, government offices and businesses. ARPANET had locations now in the UK and Norway. At this stage email was now available which scientist used to share information. Due to this TCP/IP (transmission control protocol/internet protocol) was introduced. This was so that data could be passed regardless of software and hardware on the two networked computers.

First Flight Simulator

The first Flight Simulator was developed in the 1960’s by Edwin A, Link. This programme he created replicated equations for flight controls, air craft systems, air density, turbulence, wind shear and cloud.

First Data Packet sent

The first data packet was sent in 1969.The ARPANET’s first message was sent on October 29 1969 at 10:30pm. This was the launch of the Internet.

1970’s

Email

Ray Tomlinson who worked for Bolt, Beranek and Newman invented email in 1971. At this time computers could only send messages to another user on the same computer. Ray Tomlinson created the @ character and his test email read “QWERTYUIOP”. This was a text-based message and only used between connected computers on ARPANETS system. Queen Elizabeth II was the first outside of ARPA to use email. She visited the facility and was giving the user name “HME2”. Then in 1978 email was used by Jimmy Carter in his presidential campaign. The first Spam email was sent by Gary Thuerk he used it to send a marketing message to all the employees at ARPANET in 1978.

IP Address Register

An IP Address is a unique identity number giving to everyone who uses an IP network.

Ethernet

In 1972 Robert Metcalfe invented Ethernet. The Ethernet was the network for LAN’s. It was created at Xerox PARC (Palo Alto Research Centre) with the assistance of David Bloggs.

TCP/IP

Transmission Control Protocol and Internet Protocol was introduced in the 70s.

ARPAnet in London

London had begun the research of ARPA in the NPL (national physical laboratory) by Donald Davies, he had a network that he referred to as the NPL.

1980’s

ARPAnet TCP/IP

In 1983 TCP/IP replaced ARPA’s NCP for management of the packet-switching technology. ARPA also divided the networks into military and civilian use. By the mid 80’s other networks started dominating ARPA and so it was replaced by The National Science Foundation Networks in 1986. ARPAnet was shut down in 1989. Whilst commercial and other network providers took off.

DNS

The Domain Name System was invented in1984. This meant that Domains such as .gov, .edu, .org, .net and .com were in use. This meant that the hosts of a country took two letters that indicated the country, for example Ireland use the domain .ie.

1990’s

Linux

Linux was a software system created by Zinus Torvalds. A computer operating system and free software. He released Version 1.0 of Linux in 1991.

HTTP

The HTTP is protocol for the World Wide Web (www). Tim Berners Lee wrote a Hyper-Text System. The HTML Hyper-Text Markup System is text format for the web.

HTML 1

On April 30 1993 the first HTML was released to the public domain by CERN. This page displayed the definition of the World Wide Web (w3). It gave you internal links to another page if you wanted to learn more or get answers to questions about the page.

Mosaic

The NSCA Mosaic Internet browser was the first user friendly browser released. In 1992 The National Centre for Supercomputing Applications were the first to create graphics on a web browser. This made images available on the page instead of directing you to another page linked to the web browser. This was a hyperlink. It also included “Back”, “Forward” and “Refresh” buttons on the page.

The Telegraph

In 1994 The Telegraph was the first newspaper company to release an online website called The Electronic Telegraph. The paper was displayed in plain text and small images. Small images were used so that the dial-up connection wouldn’t take too long loading, a larger image would and potentially crash the site. Its content included Internal links from Headings like “What Is BBCNC?” and “BBCNC News”. It also included an Email address to contact the company.

MP3

The Moving Picture Experts Group MPEG created digital encoding for audio and video files to be compressed and transferred. They used .m-p-3 as a domain which ran as an extension for encoding or decoding the file on which produced the sound. Karlheinz Brandenberg created this and released it to the public in 1998.

VOIP

Voice Over Internet Protocols was developed to make calls over the Internet. It revolutionised office work and companies allowing them to interact vocally over the internet to offices or businesses based in other countries.

Blog

In January 1994 a student named Justin Hall created the first online Blog, it was then called Links.net. By 1999 a programmer Peter Merholz reintroduced it as a Blog. This helped people become online bloggers of a website. On Justin’s Blog he shared his life story like an online diary. His college peers had access to this blog. His Blog was free for his audience to leave a reply as a comment or ask him questions.

CraigsList

March 1995 a man by the name of Craig Newman a computer engineer found himself out of a job. He launched a mailings list that morphed into a website known as CraigsList.org.

The Mobile Phone with Internet

The first mobile phone with internet web browsing and email was the Nokia 9000. This was a flip phone that opened up with a full keypad. It was released in 1996.

Dial-Up

Dial-up connection was made through modems. The analog signals converted to data were transmitted over telephone lines. The 90s saw the modem give connectivity through dial-up. The modem V.90 was introduced in 1998 with speeds of 56Kbps. ISDN dial-up modems came soon after with faster speeds and more reliable connections.

 

 

2000’s

ADSL

Asymmetric Digital Subscriber Line was introduced in the early 2000’s by Dr John Cioffo, a professor at electrical engineering with a PhD in programs. He built the world’s first DSL Modem. People were able to download pictures, songs, TV shows, Movies and videos from the web. The ADSL was split between telephone and internet making faster dual windows. You could be online and on a call at the same time. These new speeds of 8Mbps allowed faster download, load up and sharing to happen.

Creative Commons

This was called Creative Commons it is a free license public domain that allows content creators to copyright their work. Institutions and governments approved of this proposal while the U.S Congress opposed to the idea.

Wikipedia

Wikipedia.com was launched in 2001 by Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger. It is a free online encyclopaedia. Its content was created by qualified editors and in its first year had 12 articles published. New content was contributed by volunteers. Wikipedia was getting hundreds of visitors every day.

E-commerce

Michael Aldrich invented the groundwork for E-commerce back in 1979 with TV-shopping. Since then it had evolved into a new platform for websites to sell products and services online. It was a secure online shopping development. Key components of the website included such things as Online Storefront which listed products, images and description. The next process was the Shopping Cart/Basket allowing you to place your items in for purchase at Check Out. Wish-list would keep a product on hold to buy at a later time. The Payment Gateway Integration was to allow safe and secure online transactions which the website had setup a system for payment methods and security measures protocoled by the SSL Secure Socket Layer as encryption.

Fibre To The Cabinet

FTTP is the next step currently being worked on getting the fibre cables down to connect directly.

Cryptocurrency/Bitcoin and Blockchain

Cryptocurrency is a virtual currency online

Bitcoin

Created by Satoshi Nakamoto in 2009. The Bitcoin is currency free from Government. Satoshi worker with developers in 2010 to develop and maintain the Bitcoin network. In order for the Bitcoin to run securely on a network a Blockchain Network protocol is needed. The Blockchain Technology works by a transaction request (from a computer/device) is sent to the P2P (peer to peer) network. The network consists of Nodes, these Nodes use algorithms to validated the transaction, once the transaction is verified as cryptocurrency it processes the sender/receiver encryption of the transaction combined with other transactions (in blocks). Each block contains encryption of the value/sender/receiver and any other data with it. This is sent and the transaction is complete. As of December 2025 one Bitcoin is equal to €94,638.64.
